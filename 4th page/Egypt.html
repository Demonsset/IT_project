Ancient Egypt ⚱

A great civilization that grew along the Nile River and depended on it for life and farming
  • King:Menes - the unifier of Upper and Lower Egypt
  • Rule:The Pharaoh was considered a god and ruled the land
  • Writing 𓂀:Hieroglyphics (symbols and pictures)
  • Paper:Papyrus
  • Belief ⚱:Life after death and mummification
  • Famous ▲:Pyramids of Giza and temples
  • Science:Advanced in medicine, engineering, and astronomy

Iconic Monuments of Egypt

The Pyramids of Giza ☥

The Pyramids are one of the greatest achievements of ancient Egyptian civilization. They were built as royal tombs for the pharaohs.

The Karnak Temples ☥

Karnak Temple is the largest religious complex ever built in the ancient world. It was constructed over more than 2,000 years.

Details →
Abu Simbel Temple ☥

The Abu Simbel Temples were built by Ramesses II around 1264 BC. They were carved directly into the rock to show his power and greatness.

Philae Temple ☥

Philae Temple was dedicated to the goddess Isis and was relocated to protect it from flooding after the construction of the Aswan High Dam.

Temple of Hatshepsut ☥

The Temple of Hatshepsut was built in the 15th century BC during the reign of Queen Hatshepsut, one of the most powerful rulers of ancient Egypt.

The Citadel of Qaitbay ☥

The Citadel of Qaitbay is a historic fortress located in Alexandria, Egypt, built in the 15th century to defend the city against naval attacks.
