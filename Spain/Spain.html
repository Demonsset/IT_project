Spain

A land of passionate culture, stunning architecture, and ancient civilisations — from the Moorish palaces of Andalusia to the vibrant streets of Barcelona, Spain has captivated the world for centuries.

  • Capital: Madrid
  • Language: Spanish
  • Religion: Christianity
  • Famous For: Flamenco, bullfighting, architecture
  • Landmarks: Alhambra, Sagrada Família, Prado

🏛️ Iconic Landmarks of Spain

Alhambra Palace, Granada

A breathtaking Moorish palace and fortress overlooking Granada, renowned for its intricate Islamic architecture and lush Generalife gardens.

Further details

Sagrada Família, Barcelona

Gaudí's unfinished masterpiece — a towering basilica blending Gothic and Art Nouveau styles, under construction since 1882 and still iconic.

Further details

Park Güell, Barcelona

A whimsical public park designed by Antoni Gaudí, filled with mosaic sculptures, curved stone colonnades, and sweeping views of Barcelona.

Further details

Prado Museum, Madrid

Spain's national art museum and one of the world's finest, housing masterworks by Velázquez, Goya, and El Greco in its neoclassical halls.

Further details

Plaza Mayor, Madrid

A grand 17th-century public square in the heart of Madrid, surrounded by uniform Habsburg architecture and once used for bullfights and royal ceremonies.

Further details

Seville Cathedral

The largest Gothic cathedral in the world and the burial site of Christopher Columbus, crowned by the iconic Giralda tower — a former minaret.

Further details

Toledo, Old City

A UNESCO World Heritage city perched above the Tagus River, where Christian, Muslim, and Jewish cultures coexisted for centuries.

Further details

Montserrat Monastery

A Benedictine monastery dramatically built into the jagged peaks of Montserrat mountain near Barcelona, home to the revered Black Madonna statue.

Further details

Camino de Santiago

An ancient network of pilgrim routes across Europe converging at Santiago de Compostela, walked by hundreds of thousands each year.

Further details

Mezquita, Córdoba

A stunning mosque-cathedral with a forest of striped arches built by the Moors, later converted into a Christian cathedral — one of the most unique buildings in the world.

Further details
